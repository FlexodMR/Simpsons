Driving Model

 

Goals

 

  • More fluid and natural feel, and reward skilled/experienced players

o       Less tight and twitchy

o       More natural turning/steering

o       More sliding, finer gas/brake control

o       Less “floaty”

o       Not too loose (e.g., Wreckless)

o       Midtown Madness 2 is a good example of the driving feel

  • A sense of danger – that feeling that you are on the edge of danger when driving very fast
  • More exciting and realistic collisions
  • Don’t let the car get stuck
  • More variation between vehicles, including vehicle speed
  • Trick moves (e.g., drive on two wheels)
  • AI cars perform more like user car
  • Exciting jumps & big air
  • Balanced and tuned physics for multiplayer

 

Features

 

  • The new driving physics; the new and improved “feel”
  • Cleaner, more effective powerslides (close to the one-button touch to initiate slide of Driver)
  • Initiate sliding with physics & steering, not just with handbrake
  • Technical question – investigate possibility of “rally mode” driving on dirt surfaces
  • Analog gas/brake controls:

·        GT3 control option – right stick for full analog has/brake

·        Support two states on button gas/breaks - “hard button press” and “soft button press”

·        Experiment with using one stick for both steering and gas/brake (see XBox Bloodwake for example)

  • Full physics simulation on AI cars in accidents
  • Suspension physics or animation on nearby AI vehicles
  • Special moves:

·        2 wheel driving – some way to get the car up on 2 wheels (maybe while in Bullet Time?)

·        “Break stand” – a way of doing peel outs

·        Donuts

·        Speed Burst (see “Chase AI” document)

  • Getting unstuck & collision recovery:

·        Automatic tank mode when driving into a wall or collision volume with steering and gas pressed, and the car isn’t moving – user won’t have to reverse. See Midtown Madness 2 (with car in automatic transmission mode) for example.

·        Reverse power slides – it’s a high priority to get smooth reverse powerslides as a satisfying way to pull a 180. See GTA3 taxicabs for the quality benchmark.

  • The “Weeble effect” to make cars land upright without limited their degree of motion.
  • Exciting jumps – with the slower car, and maybe they’re heavier too, be careful not to make jumps too small

 

Tools

 

  • Tuning tools – cars can be easily tuned (with slider-like controls) in four major categories. The variables will be as independent as possible.
    • Top Speed
    • Acceleration
    • Handling/Stability
    • Mass & strength
