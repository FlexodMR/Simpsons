Animation

 

Goals

 

  • More lifelike characters
  • No “zombie” expressionless characters
  • Better presentation of animation

 

Features

 

  • Idle character animations
    • More movement, including head movement
    • Specific to each character
  • Eye blinking (under procedural control) for all characters in the game
  • Mouth flapping when speaking (except for generic pedestrians)
  • There are three classes of conversations and associated presentation:
    • Quick, minor conversations – use a variation of the gameplay camera to indicate to the user a temporary loss of character control
    • Important, longer in-game animations – full NIS with custom animation and camera work specific to the conversation
    • Eight story setups and resolutions – full FMVs with high detail character models and facial animation
  • TBD – full range of character animations needed for smooth character control

 

Content

 

Eight Walking (User Controllable) Characters

 

Each user controllable character will have animations for:

  • Idle state (doing nothing) – a very important animation that should be exaggerated (see Mario 64 and Banjo-tooie for good examples); include head-panning
    • Blink
    • Mouth flapping
    • Look around
  • Walk
  • Run
  • Turn left/right
  • Left/right 45 degree turn
  • Lean left/right (while running)
  • Decelerate and turn 180 degrees (e.g., Bart skids to a stop, turns, and runs the other way) – this animation will be specific to the character’s body type (e.g., child, woman, fat man, etc.)
  • Reaction for bumping into solid surface (wall, etc.)
  • Reaction for bumping into a character
  • Get knocked down by vehicle
  • Get up
  • Falling (off ledge)
    • Flail
  • Activate switch
    • Kid
    • Adult

 

Sixteen Drivers

 

Driver animations:

  • Turn around (when reversing the vehicle)
  • Turn left
  • Turn right
  • Decelerate
  • Accelerate
  • Wave
  • Wave goodbye
  • Idle (includes head panning)
  • Bounce in reaction to vehicle movement
  • Sway left (in reaction to vehicle movement)
  • Sway right (in reaction to vehicle movement)
  • Crash

 

Passenger Animations

 

Body Specific:

  • Point left
  • Point right
  • Point straight ahead
  • Look back
  • Look left
  • Look right
  • Get into car
  • Get out of car
  • Bounce in reaction to vehicle movement
  • Sway left (in reaction to vehicle movement)
  • Sway right (in reaction to vehicle movement)
  • Idle (include head panning)
  • Cheer
  • Crash
  • Catapult out of car
