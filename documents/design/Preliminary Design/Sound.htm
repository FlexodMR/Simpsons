13.2Voice

13.2.1  Direction

The character’s voices were a big part of the game’s success. Our goal is to improve and add to that feature.The game designers have outlined the high level design such as:

  • 3-way character conversations
  • Passenger-to-passenger interaction

·        Passenger-to-driver interaction

  • Character relationship dialog for pedestrian events
  • Characters dialog that recognizes world locations

Voice is certainly the most important sound feature if not the most important aspect of the Simpsons license itself. The technical demands for the voice in the original game were huge. We must design the voice in a way so it is more predictable in terms a sequence of events so the system knows which line of which character needs to be loaded at any given point.  Having a hierarchy such as the “must play lines” and the “optional play lines” would help make the sound system more efficient and allow us to allocate sound resources to other important sound assets.

Scheduling of the voice talent, recording and editing is the most significant dependency  of this game. We rely entirely on FOX for this and we have no control. It is imperative for FOX to schedule the voice talent recording sessions as early as possible to make sure the game will not be delayed.

The assumption is that we will receive edited sound files ready to add to the game. We will do the mastering of the voice only.

 

13.3Car sfx

13.3.1  Direction

The overall direction with the car sounds remains the same as it was in Road Rage. The intent is not to simulate a real car, but rather to create a fun and responsive driving experience.

We will be looking at improving the current engine code. We will likely re-use previous engine sounds but we will create new engine sounds appropriate for each of the new cars type and size. The main distinction will be in the performance and the car upgrade features, we will tune the cars for each incremental levels of performance.

 

13.3.2  Type definition

13.3.2.1 Engine sounds

Here are the parameters for the engine sound system

  • Trim (master volume)
  • Max volume
  • Min speed  (minimum speed required for playing a sound, i.e. surface sound of gravel will trigger only if the car is moving)
  • Pitch increase (maximum pitch increase)
  • Pitch decrease (minimum pitch increase)
  • Looping (on/ off)
  • Engine “normal” RPM (establishes the reference RPM value at which the engine sound wave will play non altered)
  • Decay time
  • Delay time
  • Attack time
  • Inside cone angle
  • Outside cone angle

13.3.3.3 Car horn

Each car will have it’s own horn sound.   There will be only one horn sound per car.  Depending on the car, one of two kinds of horn samples could be used. Some of the cars will have a one shot sample assigned to the horn.  This means that if the player holds down the horn button on the controller, the horn sound will play until it reaches the end of the sample, at which point it will stop.  The other kind of horn sound will  use a looping sample.  This will be used for some of the more obnoxious characters in the game (such as Homer or Barney).  The looping sample will allow the user to hold down the horn button and have the horn play for an indefinite period of time.

13.4.4  Tools requirements

13.4.4.1 Audio for Run-time collision physics system

The random variability of a collision simulation is graphically stunning but it is the variability that makes traditional game audio applications ineffective. A single ‘canned’ sample triggered with each simulation will never be synchronized with the graphic and seem wrong because the audio is fixed while the graphic randomly varies.

We have recently developed audio support for our run-time collision physics system. The system relays info pertinent to audio such as the behaviour of the objects, land, bounce, spin, slide, roll and also the gemometry, as which part of the object as collided. Based on that info we are then able to associate sounds with each object type and have sound matching the dynamism of the collision.

We have created a few demos of this technology, the full support and integration with the sound system is a high priority and should be done by May.

 

13.4.5  Risks / Concerns / Problems / Solutions

If the Ftech support for the run-time collision physics isn’t completed on time we would have to revert to the more traditional approach of canned collision sounds.

 

 

 

 

13.5Music

13.5.1  Direction

The music will have a more important role to play to help telling the story and support the presentation. In Road Rage, the music was more incidental and it was played back as a jukebox. This time we will associate specific music tracks with specific missions and have more music written for each of the playable characters.

Search and explore music, the in-between mission music will need to be fun foot tapping music in a style that would suit each of the 8 playable characters (very much like what Chris Tyng did for the game over short stinger in a musical style representative of each of the character, but this time in full length pieces of music)

For instance, when you play Lisa some kind of jazzy saxophone music plays until her mission kicks in, at which point a more dramatic music track would then be cued according to the mission level and intensity. There has to be a clear distinction between the search and explore mood and the mission music. We could very well re-use music from last year for the chases since it is doubtful we will be able to get 20 new songs for the game.

The personalized short stingers from Road Rage could also be re-used, new ones would need to be created for the new characters. They were created for the game over segment so we would need also need a “victory” version of each of those.  There would be eight mission failed stingers and eight victory ones – one for each controllable walking character.

13.5.2.1 In-game Music

13.5.2.1.1             Search and explore

13.5.2.1.2             Chase music

10-12 different chase type music with various level of intensity. Most tracks done for Road Rage would actually be better suited for this year’s game.

13.7Ambient and Landmark Sounds

13.7.1  Direction

We are currently considering having no music when stepping out of the car. The music would then be replaced but an ambient track such as city ambience, ocean sound with seagulls for the Squid port etc. This would help make the game more dynamic and more alive than having music throughout the game. (We could possibly use minimal and sparse underscore kind of music)

Adding landmark sounds and world based sound would greatly help to make the environment alive as well.

13.7.2  Type Definition

13.7.2.1 World based sounds

Such as water falls, nuclear generator etc. 

13.7.2.2 Landmarks sounds

Those are soundbites we will associate with specific buildings in the world. They will be about 10 seconds long, one shot samples.

 

13.7.3  Implementation / Tool requirements

13.7.3.1 World based sound

Those sounds are loaded in a buffer and played based on the proximity of the object. Those type of sounds are expected to always play (i.e. driving close to a water fall should always be heard)

 

13.7.3.2 Landmarks sounds and other ambient sounds

These sounds are also played through a buffer system however they don’t have to play every time. (probably should not, unless if it is desired as in the crow sound when entering the nuclear plant compound)  An example of as sound attached to a landmark  would be the school bell. Driving by the school you occasionally hear the school bell ringing etc. Other types would be generic sounds to be triggered randomly such as dogs barking in the suburbs, birds etc, those types are associated with a neighborhood and are triggered only if nothing else are a higher priority is required. These types of sound enhance the worlds but they are lower priority and should always be secondary if dialog is required to play.

Triggering condition: proximity to a sound making landmark + landmark id

Control: Doppler fx, 3D positional (panning)

 

13.7.4   Tools

The easiest way to implement this is to use the model developed in Dark Summit.  With the aid of a debug camera, the sound designer is able to navigate anywhere in the world and display the names of objects. Then he can edit the object file and associate a sound with any desired object. The sound gets loaded and unloaded automatically as he approaches and leaves the given object.

13.10     Additional Features

13.10.1  Reverb       

We will use reverb and other DSP capabilities to process sounds when going through tunnels. For this we will need to create groups of sfx. For instance, a car + voice of the driver (and passenger if it applies) should be grouped together so if the player goes through a tunnel, sounds triggered by that car and the voice of its occupants will be processed with reverb accordingly and independently from the music or the other sound triggered say by another player in H2H.

We now support reverb for all the platforms.

  

13.10.2                      Dolby Surround

Dolby Surround is a well-known multi-speaker playback system that requires special encoding as well as additional hardware at the user end to be able to experience it. This method is “canned”, perfect for movies but not design to be interactive.

The X-Box however, supports real-time surround encoding and this is done by the hardware. There is no extra work to do to support that feature. For example whenever  a car is behind you the sound will be automatically played through the back speakers of the surround system. Of course, to experience this feature the X-Box must be connected to an amplifier with a built-in surround sound decoder like most entertainment system and have the proper speakers setup.

The other platforms do not support it and therefore will not have real-time surround sound.
