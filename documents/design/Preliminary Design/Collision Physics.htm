Collision Physics

 

Goals

 

  • Distinguish breakables vs movables
  • Visually distinguish breakables/movables from static objects
  • Real life impact
  • Better/more user control
  • Better artist tools & consistent naming directions
  • More visually consistent crashes
  • Vehicle doesn’t get stuck
  • Allow in-game reset without world load

 

Features

 

  • Lots of breakables is good, it adds life to the world – especially if the user has a reason for hitting them
  • Breakables implemented via “delete from world and play animation”, not with physics as in Road Rage
  • A movable might also have an animation (e.g., mail box) but object itself doesn’t deform or break
  • Breakable and movables must look breakable
    • Immovable objects must look more solid – and be placed outside of common driving lanes (e.g., don’t put a big solid tree on the front lawn of a corner lot that people use as a shortcut)
  • “Tank mode” when car pressed up against collision volume (with gas and steering applied) – see Midtown Madness 2 for example
  • Realistic impacts – more rebound and shock absorption, as opposed to “two diamonds colliding”
  • More forgiving in minor collisions (e.g., where car just nicks a corner of a building and in Road Rage was completely stopped or spun around)
  • More consistent reactions to collisions in general – i.e., better correlation between user’s expected result (based on what they see) and what the physics engine produces
  • Better way of bouncing vehicle off wall (e.g., sideswipe wall at high speed) without losing so much speed
  • Less “floaty” objects
  • More variation in mass of world objects (e.g., a parking meter shouldn’t feel as heavy as a street light) – while still maintaining user advantage in these situations; the goal here is variety and a more authentic feel, not realism
    • At the same time, make sure there is NO movable object that the user can get stuck on, regardless of the object’s orientation (e.g., like the Road Rage billboards when lying flat on the ground)
  • Experiment with more verticality in collisions – e.g., realistic accident physics, but send cars up into the air
  • Reset of breakables and movables on mission restart (implies limited # of object start positions stored in a buffer when they are hit – therefore driving around the world will also cause objects in past locations to be reset in real time)
  • Artists need a better tool for ensuring consistent density of objects
  • Richer, more varied sound for collisions and vehicle accidents

 

Design Notes

 

  • Less floaty objects and more realistic collisions means greater user penalty – this means we need careful placement of heavy objects and tuning of traffic system/density
