Vehicle Chase AI

 

Goals

 

This is the area of the game where we will “make or break” our overall gameplay goal: Game has to be fun to play without Simpsons license. The essence of chase AI is that these vehicles are player enemies – so the player must have effective tools for defeating them. The goals are:

 

  • Effective strategies, tools, and counter-measures for defeating AI chase vehicles.
  • Clear strategies and tools – the user must know what they are supposed to do.
  • Visceral payoff for utilizing the strategies – use visuals, sound, controller vibration, camera (position, camera shake, etc.) and physics system (e.g., aftershock of explosion shakes player vehicle).
  • Empower the user – e.g., create difficulty by the number of enemies, not by increasing their strength.
  • Flexible AI that can be tuned by designers (e.g., location specific AI) but with runtime variables to control AI difficulty and numbers.
  • Reasonable scope

 

Prioritized Features

 

  • The golden rule – Player vehicle has a strength advantage over AI vehicles. One on one, the player can defeat an AI vehicle through sheer force.
    • An AI car will be disabled by any serious accident (e.g., impact over a certain threshold).
  • AI vehicle start locations will be placed in Maya by designers on a mission-by-mission basis. There will also be a default set of AI pawn-positions for each level.
    • These cars are “turned on” when they are a certain distance from the user, or when the user passes through a trigger volume that activates the car (question/scope – are trigger volumes really necessary, or is proximity a sufficient trigger?)
    • There are two other tiers of AI spawn points, that are linked to the first kind of AI cars, and activated when they are – but based on mission script control
      • For example, if the AI is set to “Level 2” then when basic AI vehicles are spawned, then tier 2 cars are also spawned and activated
        • If the AI is set to “level 3” then when basic AI vehicles are spawned, then tier 2 and tier 3 cars are spawned and activated
  • There are three types of AI vehicles:
    • “Generic” AI chase/escape vehicles; simple models without interiors or characters in them, there can be several of them chasing the user at once
    • “Boss” vehicles – basically a drivable vehicle, with fully rendered interior and a character in it (e.g., Wiggum in his police car). Only one of these will ever be present at one time
    • “Generic roadblock vehicles” – generic vehicles that do not drive when they are spawned, they are roadblock obstacles for the user to get around
  • Camera system that shows as much of the chase as possible – pulls back and up to show more of what’s behind you (last year’s camera system should be mostly re-usable, except for the up/down camera damping effect – when was enabled it overrode the height of the camera).
  • Driving strategies
    • Swerving through oncoming traffic greatly increases the chances of an AI accident.
    • AI vehicle line of site, or other ways you can hide from them? Maybe specific places for this, such as gas stations? If you get there when the AI can’t see you they go roaring by?
    • Outrun AI vehicles - through perfect driving and/or speed burst, or a tricky shortcut
    • Block AI vehicle with physics objects (large movables or traffic vehicles that have been knocked off their path). Has to be effective – AI should suffer a large penalty when they run into the objects.
    • Vehicle choice matters
      • Vehicles have a specialty of speed, control, or strength
  • Vehicle counter-measures. Each vehicle will have one of these abilities. They can be used three times in a mission. There are five items, listed here in priority order (i.e., it’s assumed that we’ll scope this down to the 3 or 4 best ones):
    • 1) Speed burst
    • 2) Oil slicks – causes all chasing vehicles to slide (spin out on corners, or lose steering control and/or spin out on straights).
    • 3) Bullet time – could help you make that difficult jump, or swerve through traffic to shake off the AI chasers.
    • 4) Exploding Duff keg – works like the bomb in Smuggler’s Run 2.
    • 5) Smoke screen – causes chase vehicles to stop steering while in the [huge] smoke screen.
    • HUD features – like the Smuggler’s Run 2 HUD. Three lights indicate availability and use.
    • Game logic – how are the items recharged?
      • At the beginning of all missions
      • In the game world by going to a specific location and talking to a character such as Professor Frink (probably on foot)
      • When all three are used up in “Sunday Drive” conditions (e.g., unlimited when outside of a mission, so you can practice)
  • World counter-measures:
    • Jumps that change roads (e.g., onto or off of a bridge) make the AI crash or lose you
    • Narrow alleys difficult to get into at high speed
  • Payoff for defeating AI:
    • Show the explosion/crash if we can - Bullet time in certain situations? E.g., jump escape?
    • AI Cars explode when they crash hard enough to be disabled. This has many effects:
      • Large visual explosion f/x (causes light flash you can see even if explosion is out of screen?)
      • HUGE explosion sound (think about breaking the sound barrier)
      • Camera shake
      • Controller shake
      • Aftershock hits user vehicle and causes back of vehicle to lift slightly (see bomb counter-measure effect in Smuggler’s Run 2 for example of this)
      • Vehicle parts go flying by (i.e., from behind) – an animation, not physics based
      • In convertible (e.g., Wiggum’s car) the AI driver goes flying by – animation or physics based? Harder to get approved by Fox/Gracie; so maybe lower priority?
      • Use bullet time as presentation effect in certain specific situations (e.g., you hit the jump – time goes slow, camera swings around to show police cars crashing as you fly to safety)
  • General AI rules and principles:
    • Incorporate realistic reaction times into AI programming, so that they act like humans – provides more counter-measures, and could make bullet-time in single-player mode effective.
    • No cheating – AI has same controls and physics as the player.
    • The AI will have skill levels in three different categories, adjustable by the mission scripter:
      • Reaction time/skill – low, medium, or high
      • Numbers of AI – low, medium, high (affects AI calling for backup, and the number of cars that come to help)
      • Speed of AI – Fast, Medium, Slow (definitions? E.g., are they relative to the player’s current vehicle speed?)
      • Strength
  • AI vehicle techniques to use against the player, with intended effect, and corresponding player counter-measure to prevent it from being frustrating.

Tools

 

  • Maya world-building:
    • Place start-locations and identity of AI chase vehicles. They are sitting their stationary until the user gets in a certain range, when they become active.
      • One set of start locations per mission (i.e., referenced by the mission scripter – which can also specify “no chase AI” for a mission).
      • One “fallback” set per level for non-mission situations, active when no specific mission set is referenced and “no chase AI” is not specified.
  • Mission scripter:
    • Allows setting of AI difficulty level per mission, or possibly per specific situations (e.g., go to level two when the user picks up their fourth Panda bear”).
    • Allows chase AI to be turned off
