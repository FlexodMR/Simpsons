World Building Technical Requirements                     March 8, 2002

 

 

Level design will occur to accommodate the three key in-car game play mechanics of CHASE, AVOID, and TIME, as well as the three out-of-car game play mechanics of INTERACTING with other characters, COLLECTING objects and ACTIVATING world elements.

 

Technical Requirements

 

  1. TRACK MATERIAL – Gravel roads, dirt roads, grass or ice reduces performance of driving skill. Asphalt roads and concrete roads have greater grip and coefficient of friction.
    1. Two or three driving states:

                                                               i.      Road – high friction, tight car, minimal over or under steer

                                                             ii.      Grass – low friction, lose car, more over steer, easy to ‘peel out’

                                                            iii.      Gravel – low friction, lose car, ability to steer with accelerator (rally racing style)

 

2.      ANIMATED BOUNDING VOLUMES - Animated BV’s fall into two categories of Negative and Positive. Negative animated BV’s prevent the user from completing a task. Positive animated BV’s assist in the user completing a task.

    1. All animated bounding volumes involve the following activation techniques

                                                               i.      Script – Anim. BV occurs at a predetermined time and occurrence determined by storyline level loading etc…

                                                             ii.      Trigger – a location specific switch that activates the Animated BV this trigger may have multiple states – ON, OFF, ON/OFF toggle, ON/OFF for given time allotment.  A switch may be activated out-of-car (character activation – ferry switch, industrial elevator, gate, etc.) or in-car (pass through trigger zone – door, falling boulders, etc.)

    1. Types of Animated Bounding volumes:

                                                               i.      PLATFORMS – i.e. cranes, elevators, ferries
platform is a moveable surface that provided takes the user from a specific location to another location (possibly that was previously inaccessible)

                                                             ii.      BARRIERS (vertical) – i.e. gates, doors, moving trucks
vertical barriers that restrict access to a given location. May be animated in a cycle forming a movable obstacle.

                                                            iii.      BARIERS (horizontal) – i.e. bridges, Drawbridges
horizontal barriers that allow access across a previously unaccesible location.

                                                           iv.      PATH OBJECTS – Train, Tornado, Parade, Slow moving Truck, dump truck, garbage truck, sweeper truck, windmill
Cycling immovable object with great mass the forms an obstacle to the users movement.

 

  1. FAILURE ZONES – water, cliffs will cause a failure state and require the vehicle to be re-spawned.
