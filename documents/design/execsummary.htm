Simpsons Road Rage II

Executive Summary                       01/30/02

 

 


Introduction

The aim of this document is to clearly state the high-level goals and elements of the game.  This document does not displace the game design document, but rather provides a focused and up to date overview of what has already been created

 

Game Goals

·          Fast Instant Action

·          Game has to make people laugh

·          Game has to be fun to play without Simpsons license

·          Translates the Simpsons authentically into a polished, interactive, 3D experience.

·         Stay true to the Simpsons style of humor and delivery and presentation.

 

The goal for out-of-the-car gameplay is to provide an authentic and fun Simpsons experience. The goal is thus twofold - increase the authenticity of the Simpsons experience with on-foot character and humour presentation, character interaction, and a higher level of detail in the world (e.g., the interior of the Kwik-E-Mart or Moes).  The second part of the goal is to make this part of the game fun, a substantive part of the gameplay we provide. The actions and dialogue that occur during these missions will immerse the user into the world of the Simpsons. The characters will do what they would do on the show, they’ll say what they would on the show, and the final outcome will make the user laugh, just as it would on the show. The only key here is the USER gets to control the action, and the outcome.

 

The things you can do out of the car are:

·         Get into another car (as a passenger - you stay with one character out-of-car throughout a game level)

·         Interact with another character (pre-scripted conversations, non-interactive)

·         Collect item(s) - can be an individual item such as a Squishee, or multiple items like comic books

·         Sneak past AI characters (with a simple arcade interface)

·         Chase AI characters (animals, other characters)

·         Get chased by AI characters

·         Collect animals (herd them into a vehicle)

How will we do it?

 

q       Example

 

Once a user completes a driving mission, they’ll be presented with a new “out of the car” experience. This could be an avoid-detection mission where Homer goes into the Kwik-E mart to nab the last donut. To do this he must avoid detection from APU. Using a simple line-of-sight mechanic, the user will manoeuvre Home to the donut display, hit an action button to get the donut, and control Homer out of the store without being “seen” by Apu. The transition from in the car to out of the car will be a cut scene. Once that scene has played, the character will find himself standing in the Kwik-E Mart (or other indoor/outdoor landmark) and proceed with the mission. The character will be able to talk / interact with the NPC in the mission. The user will also have limited interaction with the world, with the interactive objects being made obvious with a highlighting system.

 

Game Genre and Style

Simpsons Road Rage II is a mission based EXPERIENCE GAME. This game will be made up of both driving and out of the car elements. Simple mechanics will be employed in the “out of the car” experience. Chase / Avoid, and Collection of items.

Simply put, it takes where Road Rage left off, and adds another element of gameplay – capitalizing on the license and the humour that comes with it.

 

 

 

Simpsons Premise

The game will feature the original content and style of delivery from the Simpsons television show.  All characters will be popular, recognizable characters from the actual show.  Everyone in Springfield will be subject to some kind of weirdness at one time or another. We’ll capitalize on this. 

 

Game Premise

 The Simpsons Game-Plot Concepts

 

The following concepts are being considered for the framework story (Overall game storyline) that will tie together the 8 episodes that are intended to make up the next Simpsons Game. The storyline chosen will be referred to in the individual episodes and missions and will also be developed through Non-Interactive Sequences at the start and conclusion of the game. Each of the story ideas will heavily reference Simpsons Episodes from the first Ten Seasons as well as numerous references to Movies –with special emphasis being placed on FOX film projects.

 

1. The Case

 

Concept: Fat Tony seems to be increasing his influence and presence in town. Everyone in town is scrambling for a look inside his briefcase! Hired Goons are everywhere! Is the Mob ruling Springfield?

 

Story: In the police station, Chief Wiggum interrogates Hans Moleman, who is relating the numerous strange activities that have been taking place over the last week. Moleman indicates that Fat Tony has been acting as a representative of the strange benefactor, who keeps his identity hidden.

 

At the end of each episode, Fat Tony proceeds to pull out a briefcase, the contents of which he then shows to anyone that has paid him or helped him out with a favour. Each successive episode has the briefcase being opened a little more. Eventually, a gold glow begins showing on the amazed faces that peer into the case, although the contents are not revealed to the audience until the conclusion of the game.

 

Over the course of Moleman’s story, he relates how different characters completed tasks and collected objects for the hidden figure. Finally, Chief Wiggum releases Moleman, who then walks down the street. A distance from the station, Moleman reveals that he is indeed the mysterious benefactor and climbs into a car with his associate, Fat Tony. Fat Tony then presents Moleman with the briefcase, which he then opens.

 

Style: This story idea will reference Pulp Fiction and Ronin in that the contents of the briefcase will remain a mystery during the game. There will be a great deal of speculation, and the Simpsons character’s fascination with the case and its contents will be almost obsessive. The style will be fast paced and reference the tempo and look of the film Snatch.

 

Suggestions for contents of the briefcase: Inanimate Carbon Rod, Ring (as in Lord of the…), a Simpsons Video game (which can then be played – i.e. a surreal bonus level), film roll confirming extraterrestrial life (unexposed till Homer gets his hands on it), alternate ending to 2001 or, simply never explain what was in the case, etc.

 

 

 

 

2. Springfield Is Not Enough

 

Concept: Scorpio’s back! After seizing the east coast, Scorpio decides to continue his plot for worldwide domination! With Homer at his side and many of the Springfield citizens wooed by his impressive employment perks, could he be unstoppable?

 

Story: Scorpio sets up headquarters in Springfield. A large building with the “S” logo sits in the middle of town. While building his next Super-weapon, Scorpio begins hiring different Springfield residents to collect objects and perform tasks. As each task is completed, the Super-weapon is brought nearer to completion.

 

However, Mr. Burns, Fat Tony and Chief Wiggum are quick to realize that they are losing authority over the town and decide to foul Scorpio’s plans.

 

At the conclusion, Scorpio uses the Super-weapon to “seize the West Coast”, but then decides, after taking a closer look, that there is nothing on the West Coast worth seizing. He then offers it to Homer, who isn’t interested either.

 

Style: This story will rely heavily on references to the James Bond films, as well as the Simpsons Episode “You Only Move Twice” –starring Albert Brooks as Frank Scorpio. The focus will be on creating action/adventure-based gameplay and cinematic (within the Simpson’s world) style.

 

 

 

Game Modes

There are 2 game modes.  Default mode is Story mode

 

(a)     Story Mode

(b)     Multiplayer mode.

 

·         Story Mode is the single person experience. We will tell one of the above stories in a series of NIS’s and out of the car dialogue transactions. The user will start the game as a predermined character, and as he moves through the game, other characters will be assigned to the user. Story mode will entail a series of missions that the user must complete in order to finish the game, and ultimately – finish the story. Upon completing each area, the user can save his/her progress and pick up where they left off the next time they play.  There are various methods of gameplay in story mode:

 

Here are the mission types, and what your character will need to do in order to complete them.

 

·         Escape & Avoid

·         In-Car: AI-driven chase vehicles with varying levels of difficulty (strength, speed and numbers) chase you through dangerous, traffic-filled streets

·         Out-of-car:

·         AI-driven chase characters chasing you through maze-like environments (kind of like PacMan)

·         Sneaking puzzles; stealth - stay out of the line of sight of opponents

·         Chases

·         In-Car: Chase AI-driven cars; chase without falling behind, or chase & destroy

·         Out-of-car: Chase AI-driven characters or objects through maze-like environments

·         Collect people/animals/objects

·         In-Car & Out-of-car:

·         People and vehicles can carry multiple objects

·         Carrying capacity varies by object, vehicle and character

·         Some objects attract opponent AI attention (e.g., increase difficulty and numbers of opponents)

·         Some objects are "fragile"; i.e., crashes of a certain intensity can knock objects out of your vehicle;

·         Out-of-car:

·         People can herd live objects that are too big or awkward to carry (e.g., Panda bears, fluffy-tailed dogs, etc.)

·         Some objects are "fragile"; i.e., running while carrying too many can cause you to drop one, or lose one of your herd

 

 

 

Multiplayer mode:

 

Our multiplayer mode will consist of “capture the flag” action.  This will entail a much-improved version of this feature over the original road rage. This mode will take place in separate a level (s) of Capital City.

Our Characters will be made up of 16 main user characters. There will also be a varying number of NPC’s, Generic Pedestrians and drivers, and animals.

Each mission will revolve around a single characters and his / her interactions with others over the course of the game.
